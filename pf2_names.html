üé≤ Pathfinder 2E Specific Considerations

Ancestry Matters: Pathfinder 2E's ancestry system affects naming conventions. Elves live for centuries and may have names reflecting multiple eras, while halflings often use descriptive nicknames.

Regional Backgrounds: Your character's background can suggest naming patterns. A Noble from Cheliax might have multiple names and titles, while a Street Urchin from Absalom might only have a nickname.

Religious Influence: Many Golarion names reflect religious traditions. Followers of Sarenrae might have sun-themed names, while worshippers of Pharasma might have death-related surnames.

‚öîÔ∏è Advanced Pathfinder Naming Strategies

Ancestry-Specific Patterns

Elves: Often use flowing, musical names with natural elements. May have multiple names acquired over their long lives.

Dwarves: Clan names are crucial. Often include references to their craft, mountain homeland, or ancestral deeds.

Halflings: Descriptive nicknames are common. Birth names are often simple, but earned names reflect personality or achievements.

Humans: Most diverse, reflecting the nation of origin more than ancestry.

Social Class Indicators

Nobility: Multiple names, titles, family lineages (von, de, ibn, -ovich)

Merchants: Trade-related surnames, sometimes adopted from successful businesses

Common Folk: Simple names, often descriptive of occupation or location

Religious Naming Conventions

Many Golarion cultures incorporate religious elements into naming:

  • Sarenrae worshippers: Sun, light, dawn themes
  • Pharasma devotees: Names related to fate, prophecy, or death
  • Cayden Cailean followers: Names suggesting freedom or celebration
  • Asmodeus cultists: Often Latin-influenced with infernal overtones

üìö Golarion Historical Context

Pathfinder's world has a rich history that affects naming conventions. The Age of Darkness saw many traditional names lost, while the recent Goblinblood Wars have influenced frontier naming. The fall of Ancient Osirion means many "pharaonic" names are considered archaic, while Cheliax's diabolical turn has made infernal names more common among the nobility.

Understanding these historical layers helps create names that feel authentic to Golarion's timeline and your character's place within it.
